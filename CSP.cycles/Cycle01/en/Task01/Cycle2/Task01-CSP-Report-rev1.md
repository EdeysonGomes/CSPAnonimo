# Terminological and Scope Refinement of the Core Competency Set (Task01 – The Vending Machine)


A competência > **Develop problem-solving solutions using Finite State Machines** foi definida durante o CSP da Taks03. Percebeu-se que ela seria mais adequada à task01 e ajustou-se a relação.

Following the initial application of the **Competency Specification Process (CSP)** and its subsequent validation through the **Competency Specification Review Process (CSRP)**, an additional refinement cycle was conducted for *Task01 – Retail Vending System Scenario*.  
This iteration aimed to **reassess the granularity and terminological precision of the competency set**, ensuring closer alignment between the specified competencies, the instructional scope of the task, and the computational modeling formalism effectively exercised by learners.

As an outcome of this refinement cycle, a **targeted adjustment was introduced through the addition of a new, more specific core competency**, complementing the previously defined competency rather than replacing or reformulating it.  
The original competency remained unchanged and was expressed as:

> **Develop problem solutions using Finite Automata**

## Rationale for Refinement

While the original competency adequately captured the general requirement to design automata-based computational solutions, the use of the term **“Automata”** was identified as **overly broad and insufficiently constrained** within the specific context of Task01. In formal terms, this designation may ambiguously encompass a wide range of computational models—including, but not limited to, **Pushdown Automata** and **Turing Machines**—whose expressive power and structural characteristics **exceed the conceptual and instructional scope** of the task.

Rather than reformulating or narrowing the original competency—which was preserved for historical consistency and broader reuse—the refinement strategy adopted in this cycle consisted of **introducing an additional, more precisely scoped competency**, explicitly aligned with the modeling formalism required by the task:

> **Develop problem-solving solutions using Finite State Machines**

This newly added competency more accurately reflects the **specific computational model exercised in Task01**, while maintaining compatibility with the broader competency framework. The decision to add, rather than replace, the competency is grounded in the following considerations:

- **Task01 explicitly centers on the design, simulation, and validation of Finite State Machines (FSMs)**, encompassing both deterministic (DFA) and non-deterministic (NFA) variants, as well as their relationship with regular expressions.
- The task does not involve computational models with auxiliary memory or stack-based behavior, such as Pushdown Automata, rendering the generic term *Automata* unnecessarily inclusive for the purposes of precise competency targeting.
- The **introduction of a more granular competency** aligns with the **controlled vocabulary strategy** adopted in the CSP, which promotes terminological precision while preserving semantic interoperability across tasks.
- This adjustment directly responds to recommendations formulated during the CSRP phase to **increase specificity and reduce interpretative ambiguity**, ensuring tighter alignment between competencies, learning objectives, expected deliverables, and associated knowledge components.

It is important to emphasize that this adjustment does **not alter the conceptual intent, learning outcomes, or assessment criteria** of the original competency. Instead, it represents a **clarificatory and additive refinement**, characteristic of a post-review validation cycle, which enhances precision without compromising backward compatibility or historical traceability.

By adopting an additive refinement strategy, this cycle reinforces the CSP’s capacity to support **incremental specialization**, enabling competencies to evolve from general to task-specific formulations while remaining reusable within broader competency-based and ontology-driven educational contexts.


### Outcome of the Adjustment

The updated competence more clearly communicates the technical expectations of the task and enhances alignment with:

* The **knowledge areas** listed in the task: FSMs, Regular Expressions, and Requirements Engineering.
* The **skills expected** at the "Apply" level of Bloom’s Taxonomy.
* The broader educational objective of fostering **structured and implementable computational thinking** based on finite-state representations.

This refinement illustrates the iterative nature of the CSP, where **post-review adjustments** play a critical role in improving the relevance, clarity, and instructional alignment of the competencies defined for Problem-Based Learning (PBL) activities.
