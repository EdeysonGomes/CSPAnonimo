# **CSP-Adjustments — Task 05: *The Farmer Robot and the Feeder Robot***

## **1. Purpose of the Adjustments**

This document records the **adjustments implemented after the Expert Review (CSRP – Phase 2)** for **Task 05 – *The Farmer Robot and the Feeder Robot***. The purpose of these adjustments is to **resolve the issues formally identified in the CSRP**, ensuring full alignment with the **Competency Specification Process (CSP)** principles while preserving transparency regarding the evolution of the instructional design.

The adjustments focus on **clarifying instructional intent**, **realigning cognitive expectations**, and **qualifying the reuse of competencies** originally specified in earlier tasks, without altering the core problem scenario or invalidating prior CSP decisions.



## **2. Summary of Implemented Adjustments**

Based on the CSRP findings and recommendations, the following **four adjustment dimensions** were addressed:

1. **Explicit justification of the computational formalism**
2. **Refinement of knowledge categorization and operational scope**
3. **Realignment of learning objectives with actual cognitive demand**
4. **Qualification of competency reuse strategy**

Each adjustment is detailed below.



## **3. Adjustment 1 — Explicit Justification of the Computational Formalism**

### **Issue Identified (CSRP)**

The CSP-Report did not explicitly clarify whether the use of **Turing Machines** in Task 05 was a *minimal computational necessity* or a *deliberate instructional decision*, creating pedagogical ambiguity.

### **Adjustment Implemented**

The use of **Turing Machines** in Task 05 is now **explicitly documented as a deliberate instructional design choice**, aimed at:

* consolidating competencies previously developed with automata-based models;
* supporting **multi-agent coordination** under a unified formalism;
* introducing **computational universality** as a central abstraction.

This clarification restores continuity with earlier tasks, where **formalism adequacy** was explicitly problematized, and establishes Task 05 as a **conceptual transition point** from adequacy-driven modeling to universality-oriented reasoning.



## **4. Adjustment 2 — Refinement of Knowledge Categorization**

### **Issue Identified (CSRP)**

Certain knowledge elements—most notably the **Church–Turing Thesis**—were classified as *essential knowledge* despite not being operationally mobilized or assessable within the task.

### **Adjustments Implemented**

* The **Church–Turing Thesis** was **reclassified as theoretical background**, no longer treated as target knowledge for assessment in Task 05.
* Knowledge related to **Turing Machine Variants** was retained but **re-scoped**, ensuring that its role is:

  * **conceptual** in C08,
  * **applied** in C09,
  * **integrative/system-level** in Task 05.

These refinements improve **knowledge granularity**, strengthen **assessability**, and prevent conceptual redundancy across competencies.



## **5. Adjustment 3 — Realignment of Learning Objectives**

### **Issue Identified (CSRP)**

Learning objectives were formally correct but **understated the cognitive demand** required to complete Task 05.

### **Adjustments Implemented**

The Bloom alignment of learning objectives was revised to explicitly reflect the **higher-order cognitive processes** required by the task, including:

* **Analyze** — investigation of unified computational behavior and inter-agent interaction;
* **Apply** — adaptation of formal models to coordinated subsystems;
* **Create** — development and integration of unified modules and signaling logic.

This realignment ensures coherence between **task demands**, **competency expectations**, and **assessment criteria**, and formally positions Task 05 as a **capstone-level modeling activity**.



## **6. Adjustment 4 — Qualification of Competency Reuse**

### **Issue Identified (CSRP)**

Competencies reused from **Task 02 (C07–C09)** were enacted in **Task 05** within a **broader, more integrated, and cognitively demanding context**, without explicit acknowledgment of this scope expansion.  
As a result, the instructional demands of Task 05 risked being **under-specified**, particularly with respect to system-level reasoning, coordination, and integration.

### **Adjustments Implemented**

To address this issue, the CSP documentation now explicitly states that competencies reused in Task 05 are **not applied “as-is”**, but rather **re-enacted under expanded cognitive and contextual conditions**, characterized by:

- **multi-agent coordination**, requiring the orchestration of interacting subsystems;
- **integration of multiple behavioral modules** into a unified computational architecture;
- reliance on a **universal computational model**, supporting generality and system-level abstraction.

This qualification of reuse preserves **CSP transparency**, prevents implicit escalation of expectations, and establishes a **formal and defensible basis** for distinguishing between baseline reuse and advanced enactment.

Rather than redefining or invalidating the original competencies, this refinement adopts an **additive specialization strategy**, preserving inheritance while elevating cognitive scope.  
As a concrete outcome, the expanded enactment of competencies in Task 05 was formalized through **explicit competency specializations**, namely **C07.01, C08.01, and C09.01**, derived from their respective base competencies **C07, C08, and C09**.

### **Clarification of Specialized Competency Scope**

To ensure semantic precision and avoid overlap among the specialized competencies, the following distinctions are explicitly established:

- **C07.01 — Develop Integrated Multi-Agent Solutions Using Turing Machines**  
  This competency addresses the **construction and integration** of a multi-agent computational system, using Turing Machines as a **unified formalism** for coordinating interacting behaviors and shared control logic.

- **C08.01 — Analyze the System-Level Adequacy of Turing Machine Variants**  
  This competency focuses exclusively on the **analytical evaluation of the adequacy** of different Turing Machine variants at the **system level**, supporting informed design decisions without engaging in construction or implementation.

- **C09.01 — Apply and Coordinate Turing Machine Variants in Integrated Architectures**  
  This competency concerns the **applied coordination and assignment** of Turing Machine variants within an integrated architecture, operationalizing prior analytical judgments through structured configuration and adaptation.

Together, these competencies form a **non-overlapping and complementary progression**, moving from **analysis (C08.01)**, through **applied coordination (C09.01)**, to **integrated system construction (C07.01)**.

### **Cognitive and Conceptual Progression**

From a cognitive-process perspective, the specialization sequence in Task 05 follows a deliberate and explicit progression aligned with **Bloom’s Revised Taxonomy**:

> **Analyze (C08.01) → Apply (C09.01) → Create (C07.01)**

Conceptually, the three specializations operate at distinct but interrelated levels:

- **C08.01 — Decision Support**  
  *Which Turing Machine variant is adequate for the system requirements?*

- **C09.01 — Architectural Application**  
  *How are selected variants coordinated and assigned within the system architecture?*

- **C07.01 — System Realization**  
  *How is the integrated multi-agent solution constructed and validated as a whole?*

This explicit articulation reinforces the CSP’s capacity to support **qualified reuse, formal specialization, and progressive cognitive elevation**, while maintaining traceability and conceptual coherence across tasks.


## **7. Consolidated Status After Adjustments**

After the implementation of these adjustments:

* Task 05 remains **conceptually unchanged**, preserving its instructional narrative and problem structure;
* The **instructional intent** behind key design choices is now explicit;
* **Cognitive demand** is accurately represented;
* **Competency reuse** is formally qualified and methodologically defensible.

Task 05 is thus fully aligned with CSP principles and functions as a **culminating instructional artifact**, bridging prior automata-based modeling activities and advanced reasoning about **computational universality and system integration**.



## **8. CSP Status**

✅ **CSP-Adjusted and Aligned**

The competency model associated with *Task 05* is now considered **fully aligned with CSP standards**, with all CSRP-identified issues addressed through transparent and traceable adjustments.
