# CSP-Adjustments  
## Task202 – Team Control


## Adjustment Context

**Source of Evidence:**  
CSRP-Report – Task202 (Expert Review, Phase 2)

**Competency Involved:**  
C13 – Interpret Rule-Based Notation

**Related Competencies:**  
C12 – Develop problem-solving solutions using Pushdown Automata  
C14 – Differentiate Classifications of Formal Grammars



## 1. Identified Issue

The expert review of Task202 identified a **semantic scope tension** in the reuse of **C13 – Interpret Rule-Based Notation**.  
While the task requires learners to interpret **formal and symbolic rule systems**—specifically **context-free grammars (CFGs)** and automaton-related constraints—the competency’s current title and scope were perceived as **overly generic**.

Experts reported ambiguity regarding whether C13 refers to:
- general institutional or business rules,
- rule-based specifications in an informal sense, or
- **formal grammatical and automaton-based rule representations**.

In Task202, C13 was activated **exclusively in the context of formal language theory**, which exposed limitations in the competency’s current semantic precision.



## 2. Adjustment 


### Controlled Specialization via Derived Competency 
Introduce a **derived competency** that explicitly captures the formal-grammar-specific interpretation required in Task202, while preserving C13 as a general competency.

*Assessment:*  
Accepted. This option resolves the observed tension without destabilizing the existing competency set.



## 3. Adopted CSP-Adjustment

### 3.1 Creation of a Derived Competency

A **derived competency** is introduced to specialize the scope of C13 when formal grammatical systems are explicitly required.

**New Competency (Derived):**

> **C13.01 – Interpret Formal Grammar-Based Rules**

**Subsumption Relation:**  
C13.01 ⊑ C13


### Competency C13.01 Specification  
*(Derived from C13 – Interpret Rule-Based Notation)*



#### Competency Title

    Interpret formal grammar-based rules


#### Textual Description

This competency involves the ability to **understand, interpret, and analytically explain rule-based notations grounded in formal language theory**, with emphasis on **context-free grammars (CFGs)** as mechanisms for specifying, constraining, and reasoning about system behavior.

Learners demonstrate this competency by relating **production rules and derivation processes** to the behavior of **automata with memory**, particularly **Pushdown Automata (PDAs)**, and by explaining how grammatical rule systems encode **structural and quantitative constraints** (e.g., balance, nesting, counting) that exceed the expressive power of purely finite-state models.

The competency prioritizes **interpretative and analytical reasoning** over the construction of grammatical artifacts. It supports instructional contexts in which formal grammars are used to **justify model adequacy**, **explain system behavior**, or **document formally specified constraints**, rather than to implement or execute computational models.



#### Knowledge Specification

The following knowledge areas are essential for mastering this competency:

* **Context-Free Grammars and Formal Language Theory**  
  *Understand how context-free grammars define hierarchical and recursive symbol structures, and how production rules constrain admissible strings or event sequences.*

* **Pushdown Automata as Recognizers of CFGs**  
  *Understand the correspondence between grammatical formalisms and automaton models, particularly the role of stack-based memory in recognizing and explaining context-free structures.*

* **Formal Reasoning in Computation Theory**  
  *Understand how grammatical derivations, symbolic rules, and automaton transitions jointly support the formal specification, explanation, and validation of system behavior.*



#### Disposition Specification

The interpretation of formal grammar-based rule systems is supported by the following professional dispositions:

* **Analytical Rigor** – Approach formal grammatical descriptions with precision and attention to correctness.
* **Responsibility to Formal Consistency** – Ensure that interpretations faithfully reflect the constraints expressed by grammatical rules.
* **Reflective Reasoning** – Examine alternative grammatical representations and assess their adequacy for explaining system behavior.
* **Collaborative Reasoning** – Engage in peer-based discussion and review to validate interpretations of formal rule systems.

These dispositions support disciplined reasoning in theory-intensive tasks without constituting independent assessment targets.



#### Knowledge–Skill Pairing

##### Mapping Knowledge to Skills

To demonstrate this competency, learners must be able to:

* **Interpret context-free grammars** in order to explain how production rules constrain admissible behaviors, inputs, or event sequences.
* **Relate grammatical rules to automaton behavior**, particularly stack operations and state transitions in Pushdown Automata.
* **Analyze formal rule systems** to identify structural properties, expressive limitations, or sources of ambiguity relevant to system modeling and validation.



#### Bloom’s Taxonomy Alignment

This competency primarily engages the following cognitive levels:

* **Understand** – Recognize and explain the structure, purpose, and meaning of grammar-based rule systems.
* **Analyze** – Examine grammatical rules and relate them to automaton behavior and system constraints.



#### Verb Annotation

Interpret · Explain · Analyze · Relate · Examine

#### Subsumption Note

**C13.01** is a **controlled specialization** of **C13 – Interpret Rule-Based Notation**, restricted to tasks that explicitly mobilize **formal grammatical systems grounded in computation theory**.  
While **C13** addresses the interpretation of rule-based notations in a general, domain-independent manner, **C13.01** is activated when instructional contexts require **formal language theory, grammatical reasoning, and explicit correspondence with automaton models**.



### 3.2 Scope of C13.01

C13.01 focuses on the ability to:

- interpret **context-free grammars (CFGs)** and other formal grammatical representations;
- relate grammatical rules to **automaton behavior**, particularly stack-based operations in PDAs;
- explain how symbolic rules enforce structural or quantitative constraints in computational models.

This competency is activated when **formal language theory** is a central explanatory mechanism, as in Task202.



### 3.3 CSP Usage Rule

The CSP is adjusted with the following procedural rule:

- **C13** should be reused when tasks involve interpretation of **general rule-based specifications** (e.g., institutional rules, symbolic policies, abstract rule sets).
- **C13.01** should be reused when tasks explicitly require interpretation of **formal grammatical systems** linked to automata or language classes.

This distinction must be documented in the **Competency Specification** and, when applicable, reviewed in the corresponding **CSRP-Report**.



## 4. Ontological Impact ([Ontology])

From an ontological perspective, this adjustment:

- preserves **semantic stability** of the original competency C13;
- introduces a **specialized subclass** aligned with formal language theory;
- supports more precise reasoning about competency reuse, activation context, and subsumption.

The adjustment is compatible with [Ontology]’s support for:
- competency hierarchies,
- reuse through subsumption,
- context-sensitive activation.



## 5. Expected Impact

The adoption of this CSP-Adjustment is expected to:

- eliminate ambiguity in the reuse of C13 in theory-intensive tasks;
- improve transparency and rigor during expert review;
- prevent semantic overload in broadly defined competencies;
- strengthen alignment between **task demands**, **formal knowledge**, and **competency semantics**.
