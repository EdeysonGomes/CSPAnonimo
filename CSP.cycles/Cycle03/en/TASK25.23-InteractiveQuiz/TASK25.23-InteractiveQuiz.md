# TASK25.5: Interactive Quiz – Fundamental Concepts of Hardware and Software


## 1. Activity Context

The activity was carried out with the **1st-year A and B classes of the [TECHNICAL_PROGRAM] at [INSTITUTION]**, after completing the theoretical block on **computer architecture**, covering hardware, software, and the basic organization of computational systems.

With the goal of **reviewing, strengthening, and applying essential knowledge**, the instructor adopted an **interactive quiz on [QUIZ_PLATFORM]**, a modality that promotes student engagement, healthy competition, and meaningful learning through active participation.

The activity contributed to:

* **Diagnosing students’ understanding and difficulties** prior to the formal assessment;
* **Consolidating key concepts** in a playful and motivating format;
* **Developing social skills**, such as communication, argumentation, and collaboration;
* **Promoting immediate feedback**, supporting responsive and adaptive learning.

## 2. Task Statement

Students participated in an **interactive quiz of 15 multiple-choice questions**, based on the content taught in class and the competencies assessed in the written exam.

The questions covered:

* Identification, function, and characteristics of **hardware** components (CPU, memory, input/output devices);
* Conceptual and functional differences between **system software** and **application software**;
* Relationships between **hardware, software, and computer performance**;
* Problem-based scenarios involving assembly and operation of **real computational systems**.

The activity took place in the **computer laboratory**, using both **mobile phones** and **computers**. Answers were submitted in real time, with automatic scoreboards updated by [QUIZ_PLATFORM], encouraging **healthy competition** and sustained student engagement.

After each question, the instructor led a **guided discussion**, explaining the correct answers, clarifying doubts, and relating the explanations back to the theoretical lessons.

## 3. Technical and Pedagogical Requirements

For effective implementation of the activity, the following requirements were considered:

### Technical Requirements

* Use the **[QUIZ_PLATFORM]** platform for quiz creation, administration, and performance monitoring;
* Ensure access to devices (computers or mobile phones) with internet connectivity;
* Verify network stability and proper functioning of the equipment.

### Pedagogical Requirements

* Create questions aligned with the content of **hardware, software, architecture, and computer organization**;
* Ensure equitable participation, accessibility, and inclusion of all students;
* Promote **immediate feedback** after each round of answers;
* Encourage argumentation, communication, and collaborative correction;
* Record evidence of individual and group participation, observing engagement and ethical behavior.

## 4. Activity Modality and Assessment Criteria

### Activity Modality

* **Nature:** Summative and formative;
* **Format:** Individual performance with collaborative discussion mediated by the instructor.

### Assessment Criteria

1. **Technical Performance**

   * Number of correct answers and response time on the quiz;
   * Conceptual coherence demonstrated in the answers.

2. **Participation and Argumentation**

   * Engagement in the post-question discussions;
   * Ability to justify choices and help clarify peers’ doubts.

3. **Conceptual Mastery**

   * Appropriate use of technical vocabulary (hardware, software, architecture, performance);
   * Clarity and accuracy when explaining concepts.

4. **Attitudes and Behaviors**

   * Collaboration, respect, ethical conduct, and engagement throughout the activity;
   * Contribution to a positive and participatory learning environment.
