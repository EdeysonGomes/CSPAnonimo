# **Specialized Competency Specification — C09.01**

## **Competency C09.01 — Specialized Form of C09**


## **1. Specialization Statement (Formal Justification)**

**Competency C09.01** is a **specialization of Competency C09 – *Make Use of Turing Machine Variants***, enacted in the context of **Task 05 – *The Farmer Robot and the Feeder Robot***.

While **C09** focuses on the **applied use of Turing Machine variants** to improve clarity or adequacy in **locally scoped problem-solving scenarios**, **C09.01** captures an **expanded and specialized form of application**, in which learners must **select, adapt, and coordinate variants within an integrated, multi-agent computational system**.

This specialization is required because Task 05 demands **system-level modeling decisions**, where the use of variants is no longer local or isolated, but **architecturally consequential**, directly affecting coordination, interaction, and global system behavior.



## **2. Competency Title**

    Apply and Coordinate Turing Machine Variants in Integrated Architectures


## **3. Textual Description (Specialized)**

This competency involves the ability to **select, adapt, and coordinate the use of Turing Machine variants**—such as **multi-tape** or **non-deterministic models**—within **integrated and multi-agent computational systems**.

Learners must be able to **analyze system-level requirements**, determine how different variants can be combined or assigned to specific subsystems, and **apply these variants in a coordinated manner** to support interaction, information flow, and shared computational behavior.

Demonstrating this competency requires **applied architectural reasoning**, in which the use of variants is **justified by global constraints** (e.g., coordination, modularity, or signaling needs), rather than by local convenience alone. The competency deliberately avoids full system construction or metatheoretical discussion, focusing instead on **applied and justifiable use of variants within an integrated design**.



## **4. Cognitive Scope and Bloom Alignment**

| Aspect               | C09 (Base)               | C09.01 (Specialized)                           |
| -------------------- | ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Dominant Bloom Level | **Apply**                | **Apply + Analyze**                            |
| Modeling Scope       | Local / single-model use | Integrated, multi-agent coordination           |
| Decision Nature      | Variant use for adequacy | Variant coordination with architectural impact |
| Relation to C07.01   | Feeds construction       | Prepares and constrains integration            |

C09.01 explicitly **extends the cognitive scope** of C09 by requiring **Analyze** as a supporting process, necessary for coordinating variant use across interacting subsystems.



## **5. Knowledge Specification (Specialized)**

The following knowledge areas are required to demonstrate **C09.01**, in addition to those inherited from **C09**:

* **Turing Machine Variants (Integrated Use Perspective)**
  Knowledge of how different Turing Machine variants can be **assigned, adapted, and coordinated** across multiple subsystems, considering their **structural implications for interaction and information flow**.

* **System-Level Requirements Engineering**
  Ability to interpret and formalize **global constraints** (e.g., coordination rules, shared memory assumptions, signaling protocols) that guide the applied use of variants in an integrated system.

* **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)**
  Capacity to analyze interactions between subsystems, reason about the consequences of variant selection, and justify coordinated modeling decisions.



## **6. Knowledge–Skill Pairing (Specialized)**

### **Mapping Knowledge to Skills**

To demonstrate this competency, learners must be able to:

* **Apply** Turing Machine variants by **assigning and adapting models** to specific subsystems within a multi-agent architecture.

* **Analyze** system interactions to determine how the coordinated use of variants affects **consistency, synchronization, and global behavior**.

* **Apply** system-level **Requirements Engineering** to translate global constraints into **variant selection and coordination decisions**.

* **Analyze** **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)** to justify architectural trade-offs and evaluate the adequacy of the coordinated design.


## **7. Verb Annotation (Specialized)**

* **Apply** → *Turing Machine Variants (integrated use)*
  **Verbs:** *Select, Adapt, Coordinate*

* **Analyze** → *System Interaction*
  **Verbs:** *Examine, Relate, Assess*

* **Apply** → *Requirements Engineering*
  **Verbs:** *Interpret, Align, Constrain*



## **8. Relationship to Base Competency ([Ontology] Perspective)**

Formally:

```text
C09.01 ⊑ C09
```

Where:

* **C09** defines the **applied capability** to use Turing Machine variants for improved modeling adequacy;
* **C09.01** specializes this capability for **coordinated, system-level application** in **multi-agent and integrated contexts**.

All knowledge and skills of C09 are **inherited**, with **additional constraints and elevated analytical demands** introduced by the specialization.
