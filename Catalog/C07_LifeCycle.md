# Ciclo de Vida da Competência C07 - *Develop Problem-Solving Solutions Using Turing Machines*


## 1. Definição Original — *CSP-Report (Ciclo 1)*

**Origem:**
A competência C07 emerge diretamente da análise da tarefa *Traffic Control*, que exige:

* interpretação de requisitos reais (Anonymous),
* tradução para especificações formais,
* construção de um sistema computacional completo baseado em **Turing Machines**.

**Evidência instrucional:**

* necessidade de classificar, contar e decidir com base em dados de sensores;
* impossibilidade de resolver o problema apenas com reconhecimento ou teste de modelos.

➡️ **Resultado:** identificação da necessidade de uma competência de **desenvolvimento de soluções formais**, não apenas de compreensão.



### Primeira Especificação (CSP Phase 1B: Competency Authoring)


### Competency Title
    Develop Problem-Solving Solutions Using Turing Machines

### Textual Description  
Design and implement a Turing Machine that processes and classifies vehicle data, transforming user requirements into a functional model. Validate the model through simulation and ensure alignment with practical constraints.


### Knowledge Specification
The following knowledge areas are critical for this competency:  

- **Turing Machines (nmT):**
    - Essential to achieving the overall objective, this knowledge involves understanding and applying the principles of Turing Machines to model the solution for traffic categorization and analysis.

- **Requirements Analysis:**
    - Necessary to accurately identify and formalize the needs of Domain stakeholders, ensuring that system requirements are well understood and properly implemented.

- **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK):**
    - Fundamental for planning and developing the solution, enabling students to analyze available information, evaluate alternative strategies, and propose approaches that balance accuracy and efficiency.

### Disposition Specification

The analysis of the **A highway traffic monitoring scenario*** task highlights key behavioral dispositions required to solve the problem and deliver an effective solution. The main dispositions include:

**Collaborative**
- Work closely with team members to design and integrate all parts of the system, share insights, and ensure a cohesive solution.

**Responsible**
- Demonstrate commitment to the quality of the solution, meeting deadlines, and ensuring that Anonymous's technical requirements are fully addressed.

**Proactive**
- Anticipate potential implementation challenges, propose improvements, and adapt the computational model to increase the accuracy of vehicle classification.

**Inventive**
- Explore different Turing Machine variants and their applicability to traffic control, analyzing the most effective ways to process sensor data from the highway.

**Creative**
- Develop innovative solutions to enhance the nighttime traffic data analysis, allowing the system to become more efficient and responsive to infrastructure management needs.

### Knowledge-Skill Pairing

This step maps **knowledge areas to the corresponding skills** required to successfully demonstrate competency in this task.  

#### Mapping Knowledge to Skills

To achieve this competency, students must demonstrate the ability to: 

- **Apply** *Analytical and Critical Thinking* along with knowledge of *Turing Machines* to develop a computational solution that processes and classifies traffic data. This includes modeling sensor behavior and vehicle categorization within the formal structure of a Turing Machine.

- **Construct** *Turing Machines* to represent and simulate the logic of counting and categorizing vehicles, ensuring that the model correctly processes the data and delivers an efficient solution to the problem.

- **Apply** *Requirements Analysis* to correctly identify and specify the essential requirements of the system. Students must elicit Anonymous’s needs, interpret sensor data, and define criteria for traffic analysis, ensuring that user requirements are translated into practical functionalities in the developed solution.


#### Bloom’s Taxonomy Alignment

To ensure a structured and progressive learning approach, each knowledge component is aligned with Bloom’s Revised Taxonomy, providing a clear framework for competency assessment.

- **MTuring Machines - Create**
- Create level is used to assess students’ ability to employ the concept of Turing Machines in modeling and developing a solution to the traffic control problem.

- **Requirements Analysis - Apply**
- Apply level is used to evaluate whether students can interpret and structure system requirements based on the problem’s needs.

- **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK) - Apply**
- Apply level is selected to assess students’ ability to break down the problem into manageable components and develop effective strategies for solving it.


#### Verb Annotation
To provide clarity on competency expectations, the following verb annotations define the required actions:  

**Develop** a formal Turing Machine: states, tape alphabet, head movements, and transition function.
**Construct** and simulate the TM to model realistic traffic light or vehicle queue behaviors.
**Invent** enhancements to demonstrate adaptability and originality.

#### **Turing Machines – Create**

  **Verbs**: *Develop*, *Invent*, *Construct*


#### **Requirements Analysis – Apply**

* **Interpret** the functional and non-functional needs described in the problem.
* **Organize** those needs into formal specifications.
* **Apply** principles of requirement modeling to guide system design.

 **Verbs**: *Interpret*, *Organize*, *Apply*


#### **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK) – Apply**

* **Decompose** the system into manageable logical components.
* **Identify** key variables and interactions.
* **Apply** structured reasoning to map problem characteristics into formal models.

 **Verbs**: *Decompose*, *Identify*, *Apply*

## **2. Revisões — *CSRP-Report (Ciclo 1 — Revisão por Especialistas)***

Durante a **Revisão por Especialistas (CSRP – Ciclo 1)**, a competência **C07 – Develop problem-solving solutions using Turing Machines** foi analisada por meio de uma **avaliação técnico-pedagógica baseada exclusivamente em artefatos educacionais**. A revisão concentrou-se na **coerência conceitual**, na **adequação cognitiva** e na **operacionalidade instrucional** da especificação da competência no contexto da *Task 02*.

### **Principais achados do CSRP**

* **Identificação de conteúdo teórico não operacional**
  Foi identificada a presença de **conteúdo metateórico não mobilizado pela tarefa**, em particular a **Church–Turing Thesis**, cuja inclusão não contribui diretamente para a demonstração observável da competência, uma vez que não é requerida para a **construção, simulação ou validação** da Turing Machine proposta.

* **Necessidade de reorientação para a execução do modelo**
  A revisão apontou a necessidade de **reorientar a especificação da competência para o desempenho efetivo**, enfatizando atividades de **modelagem formal, implementação, simulação e verificação do comportamento** da Turing Machine, em detrimento de discussões teóricas de natureza justificativa ou histórica.

* **Confirmação do enquadramento cognitivo e do verbo nuclear**
  Os especialistas confirmaram a **adequação do verbo nuclear *Develop*** e do **nível cognitivo predominante *Create*** (Taxonomia de Bloom Revisada), reconhecendo que a competência demanda **produção de artefatos formais funcionais**, coerentes com a natureza construtiva e produtiva da tarefa.

### **Decisão técnica do CSRP**

Com base nos achados, foram estabelecidas as seguintes diretrizes de ajuste:

* **Refinar os conhecimentos associados à competência**, removendo elementos não exercitados no contexto da tarefa e mantendo apenas aqueles **estritamente necessários à modelagem, execução e validação** de Turing Machines;
* **Reforçar explicitamente o caráter produtivo e aplicado da competência**, consolidando a C07 como uma competência centrada na **resolução de problemas por meio de modelagem formal**, e não na discussão metateórica de fundamentos da computação.

Essas decisões forneceram o **fundamento técnico e pedagógico** para os ajustes subsequentes realizados na fase de **CSP-Adjustments**, preservando a integridade histórica da especificação original e viabilizando sua estabilização conceitual e futura especialização.









## **3. Alterações Implementadas — *CSP-Adjustments***

Com base nas recomendações consolidadas no **CSRP-Report (Ciclo 1)**, foram implementados **ajustes direcionados e não expansivos** na competência **C07**, com o objetivo de **estabilizar seu núcleo semântico**, reforçar sua **operacionalidade instrucional** e garantir **coerência entre conhecimento, habilidade e nível cognitivo**, preservando o escopo fundacional da *Task 02*.

### **Ajustes implementados**

* **Migração do referencial de conhecimento para CS2013**, adotando uma taxonomia **mais granular, hierárquica e educacionalmente orientada**, adequada à especificação e avaliação de competências;
* **Substituição de *Requirements Analysis* por *Requirements Engineering***, alinhando a terminologia ao uso consolidado na área e refletindo de forma mais precisa as atividades de **elicitação, especificação e validação de requisitos**;
* **Remoção de conhecimentos não exercitados pela tarefa**, em especial conteúdos de natureza metateórica, assegurando que todos os elementos declarados sejam **operacionalmente mobilizados e observáveis**;
* **Alinhamento fino entre conhecimento, habilidade e verbo Bloom**, garantindo coerência entre o **desempenho esperado**, o **nível cognitivo (*Create*)** e os **critérios de avaliação**.

Esses ajustes reforçam o papel da fase de *CSP-Adjustments* como um mecanismo de **consolidação conceitual**, e não de redefinição histórica da competência.



## **Competency C07 Specification — *CSP-Adjustments***

### **Competency Title**

  Develop problem-solving solutions using Turing Machines


### **Textual Description — Revised**

This competency refers to the ability to **design, construct, and validate complete problem-solving solutions** using **Turing Machines** as a formal model of computation. It involves **eliciting and structuring system requirements**, translating them into **precise machine-level specifications**, and **constructing executable Turing Machine models** that correctly solve well-defined computational problems.

Learners are expected to **define states, tape alphabets, transition functions, and halting conditions** in strict accordance with the problem specification, ensuring **logical correctness, completeness, and functional adequacy** of the resulting machine. Solutions must be **validated through execution or simulation**, demonstrating correct behavior across all relevant input cases.

Within the scope of this competency, emphasis is placed on the **productive application of formal reasoning and computational modeling**, focusing on the **operational use of Turing Machines** to represent, implement, and validate solutions. The competency deliberately avoids abstract or metatheoretical discussion, preparing students to employ Turing Machines as a **concrete problem-solving instrument** within theoretical computer science and algorithmic analysis.




## **Knowledge Specification**

The following knowledge areas are essential for demonstrating this competency:

* **Turing Machines (nmT)**
  Knowledge of the **formal structure and operational semantics** of Turing Machines, including states, tape alphabets, transition functions, head movement, and halting conditions. This knowledge enables the **construction, execution, and validation of executable machine models** that correctly realize specified computational behavior.

* **Requirements Engineering**
  Knowledge of techniques for **eliciting, structuring, and formalizing system requirements**, including the identification of inputs, constraints, and expected behaviors. This knowledge supports the translation of real-world problem statements into **precise, machine-level specifications** suitable for implementation using formal computational models.

* **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)**
  Capacity to **decompose complex problems**, identify relevant variables and constraints, and reason systematically about solution strategies within a **formal modeling context**. This knowledge underpins decision-making during model construction, validation, and refinement, ensuring logical coherence and correctness.



## **Disposition Specification**

The *Traffic Control* task requires behavioral dispositions that support effective formal modeling and problem solving:

* **Collaborative** — work constructively with peers during analysis, modeling, and validation phases;
* **Responsible** — ensure correctness, completeness, and adherence to problem specifications;
* **Proactive** — anticipate modeling challenges and propose improvements to the solution;
* **Inventive** — explore alternative modeling strategies within the expressive power of Turing Machines;
* **Creative** — design efficient and coherent machine representations aligned with task constraints.



## **Knowledge–Skill Pairing**

This section establishes an explicit mapping between the **knowledge areas** required by the competency and the **observable skills** that characterize successful learner performance, ensuring coherence between **knowledge, action, and assessment**.

### **Mapping Knowledge to Skills**

To demonstrate this competency, learners must be able to:

* **Create** computational solutions by applying **Turing Machine** concepts to **design, construct, and validate executable models** that implement problem-specific behaviors (e.g., classification, recognition, or counting) within a formal machine structure.

* **Apply** **Requirements Engineering** to **elicit, structure, and formalize system requirements**, ensuring that problem constraints, inputs, and expected behaviors are accurately reflected in the resulting Turing Machine specification.

* **Apply** **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)** to **decompose the problem**, identify relevant variables and constraints, select appropriate modeling strategies, and **justify design decisions** throughout the solution development and validation process.



## **Bloom’s Taxonomy Alignment**

Each knowledge component is explicitly aligned with **Bloom’s Revised Taxonomy**, supporting transparent and structured assessment:

* **Turing Machines — Create**
  Assesses the learner’s ability to **design, construct, execute, and validate** Turing Machine models that correctly solve the specified problem.

* **Requirements Engineering — Apply**
  Evaluates the ability to **interpret, organize, and translate** problem requirements into formal, machine-level specifications.

* **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK) — Apply**
  Assesses the ability to **analyze the problem context**, decompose it into manageable components, and reason systematically about modeling and solution strategies.



## **Verb Annotation**

The following action verbs clarify the expected performance level for each knowledge–skill pairing:

* **Turing Machines — Create:** *Design, Construct, Develop, Validate*
* **Requirements Engineering — Apply:** *Interpret, Organize, Specify*
* **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK) — Apply:** *Decompose, Identify, Justify*
