# **Specialized Competency Specification — C13.01 (Task202)**

### Competency C13.01 Specification  
*(Derived from C13 – Interpret Rule-Based Notation)*



#### Competency Title

    Interpret formal grammar-based rules


#### Textual Description

This competency involves the ability to **understand, interpret, and analytically explain rule-based notations grounded in formal language theory**, with emphasis on **context-free grammars (CFGs)** as mechanisms for specifying, constraining, and reasoning about system behavior.

Learners demonstrate this competency by relating **production rules and derivation processes** to the behavior of **automata with memory**, particularly **Pushdown Automata (PDAs)**, and by explaining how grammatical rule systems encode **structural and quantitative constraints** (e.g., balance, nesting, counting) that exceed the expressive power of purely finite-state models.

The competency prioritizes **interpretative and analytical reasoning** over the construction of grammatical artifacts. It supports instructional contexts in which formal grammars are used to **justify model adequacy**, **explain system behavior**, or **document formally specified constraints**, rather than to implement or execute computational models.



#### Knowledge Specification

The following knowledge areas are essential for mastering this competency:

* **Context-Free Grammars and Formal Language Theory**  
  *Understand how context-free grammars define hierarchical and recursive symbol structures, and how production rules constrain admissible strings or event sequences.*

* **Pushdown Automata as Recognizers of CFGs**  
  *Understand the correspondence between grammatical formalisms and automaton models, particularly the role of stack-based memory in recognizing and explaining context-free structures.*

* **Formal Reasoning in Computation Theory**  
  *Understand how grammatical derivations, symbolic rules, and automaton transitions jointly support the formal specification, explanation, and validation of system behavior.*



#### Disposition Specification

The interpretation of formal grammar-based rule systems is supported by the following professional dispositions:

* **Analytical** – Approach formal grammatical descriptions with precision and attention to correctness.
* **Responsible** – Ensure that interpretations faithfully reflect the constraints expressed by grammatical rules.
* **Reflective** – Examine alternative grammatical representations and assess their adequacy for explaining system behavior.
* **Collaborative** – Engage in peer-based discussion and review to validate interpretations of formal rule systems.

These dispositions support disciplined reasoning in theory-intensive tasks without constituting independent assessment targets.



#### Knowledge–Skill Pairing

##### Mapping Knowledge to Skills

To demonstrate this competency, learners must be able to:

* **Understand** context-free grammars to interpret how production rules constrain admissible structures and behaviors.

* **Understand** Pushdown Automata as recognizers of CFGs, relating grammatical derivations to stack operations and state transitions.

* **Analyze** formal rule systems using formal reasoning in computation theory to identify structural properties, expressive limits, and possible ambiguities.



#### Bloom’s Taxonomy Alignment

This competency primarily engages the following cognitive levels:

* **Understand** – Recognize and explain the structure, purpose, and meaning of grammar-based rule systems.

* **Analyze** – Examine formal rule systems, relate grammatical structures to automaton behavior, and identify expressive limits, structural properties, or possible ambiguities.

#### Subsumption Note

**C13.01** is a **controlled specialization** of **C13 – Interpret Rule-Based Notation**, restricted to tasks that explicitly mobilize **formal grammatical systems grounded in computation theory**.  
While **C13** addresses the interpretation of rule-based notations in a general, domain-independent manner, **C13.01** is activated when instructional contexts require **formal language theory, grammatical reasoning, and explicit correspondence with automaton models**.
