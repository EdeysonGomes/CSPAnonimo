# Competency Lifecycle — C11 – *Identify Patterns in Finite State Machines*



## 1. Definição Original — *CSP-Report (Ciclo 1)*

### **Original Intent**

The initial definition of C11 aimed to develop learners’ ability to **recognize recurring structures and behavioral regularities in Finite State Machines (FSMs)**. The competency was introduced to support higher-level reasoning about FSM models beyond their mere construction, focusing on abstraction, interpretation, and structural analysis.

At this stage, the competency sought to:

* Encourage examination of **state–transition structures**;
* Promote recognition of **recurrent patterns** in FSM behavior;
* Support interpretative reasoning about model structure and complexity.

### **Identified Characteristics (Ciclo 1)**

* Conceptual focus on FSM analysis, but with **limited operational clarity**;
* Overlap with modeling competencies, without clear differentiation;
* Bloom level implied (*Analyze*), but not explicitly articulated.


### Primeira Especificação (CSP Phase 1B: Competency Authoring)


### Competency C11 Specification

### Competency Title

  Infer and Identify Patterns in Finite State Machines


### Textual Description

Analyze the structure and behavior of **Finite State Machines (FSMs)** to draw inferences and identify emerging patterns that influence model complexity and performance. This competency involves recognizing relationships between input classifications, state transitions, and the number of states required for effective modeling.

Students are expected to demonstrate the ability to **analyze FSMs**, detect design regularities or redundancies, and apply **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)** to refine and interpret system behavior.


### Knowledge Specification

The following knowledge areas are critical for this competency:

* **Finite State Machines**
  * Understand structural properties of FSMs, including states, transitions, and minimization principles, and how these influence computational modeling.

* **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)**
  * Apply logical reasoning to identify hidden structures, draw meaningful conclusions, and justify design decisions based on pattern recognition and model analysis.



### Disposition Specification

Given the abstract nature of the task and the cognitive demands of pattern recognition and inference, the following behavioral dispositions are essential:

* **Inventive**
  * Approach FSM analysis with originality, exploring non-obvious or alternative interpretations of structural behavior.

* **Creative**
  * Devise innovative methods to visualize, interpret, or generalize state-based patterns.

* **Meticulous**
  * Pay careful attention to detail when identifying similarities, redundancies, or inefficiencies in state design and transitions.


### Knowledge-Skill Pairing

This section maps the **knowledge areas** to the corresponding **skills** required to successfully demonstrate this competency.

#### Mapping Knowledge to Skills

To demonstrate this competency, students must:

* **Analyze** knowledge of **Finite Automata** to examine state structures, recognize patterns, and determine how input categories influence the number of states and transitions required.
* **Apply** knowledge of **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)** to make informed inferences, detect behavioral regularities, and refine FSM designs based on logical reasoning and task-specific constraints.



#### Bloom’s Taxonomy Alignment

The **Analyze** level of Bloom’s Taxonomy is used to assess learners' ability to examine components of FSMs, explain their functional behavior, and justify structural or behavioral improvements based on evidence and reasoning.



#### Verb Annotation

To clarify expectations, the following verbs are aligned with the knowledge areas at the Analyze level:

* **Analyze** → Finite Automata → *Analyze, Evaluate, Compare*

## **2. Revisões — *CSRP-Report (Ciclo 1 — Revisão por Especialistas)***

### **Key Reviewer Findings**

During the expert review phase, the following issues and opportunities were identified:

* **Ambiguity in competency title and scope**
  The original phrasing was considered potentially confusing, as it did not clearly distinguish **pattern identification** from **FSM construction or optimization**.

* **Insufficient specification of observable learner actions**
  Reviewers noted that the competency lacked explicit articulation of what constitutes successful performance (e.g., types of patterns to be identified or how findings should be justified).

* **Need for clearer separation from productive competencies**
  C11 required clearer positioning as an **analytical competency**, complementary to but distinct from FSM design and implementation.

### **Reviewer Recommendations**

* Retain the competency, but **clarify its analytical nature**.
* Reformulate the description to emphasize **examination, comparison, and interpretation** of FSM structures.
* Explicitly align the competency with the **Analyze** level of Bloom’s Revised Taxonomy.
* Adjust associated knowledge to focus on **Finite State Machines** and **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)**.

### **Decision Threshold**

* **Needs Revision**



## **3. Alterações Implementadas — *CSP-Adjustments***

### **Implemented Refinements**

Based on the CSRP recommendations, the following adjustments were implemented:

* **Scope clarification**

  * The competency was explicitly defined as an **analytical competency**, focused on identifying structural and behavioral patterns rather than constructing FSMs.

* **Textual refinement**

  * The description was revised to specify observable actions such as *examining state architectures*, *comparing transition structures*, and *interpreting recurring patterns*.

* **Knowledge alignment**

  * Knowledge components were refined to:

    * **Finite State Machines**, emphasizing structure, equivalence, and minimization;
    * **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)**, supporting pattern recognition and justification.

* **Bloom alignment**

  * Primary cognitive level explicitly set to **Analyze**, with supporting **Apply** actions for reasoning and justification.



## **Competency C11 Specification**

### **Competency Title**

    Identify Patterns in Finite State Machines


### **Textual Description**

This competency focuses on the ability to **analyze the structure and behavior of Finite State Machines (FSMs)** in order to identify recurring patterns, regularities, and redundancies that influence **model complexity, efficiency, and expressiveness**. It involves examining how input classifications, transition structures, and state configurations shape system behavior and reveal opportunities for abstraction or optimization.

Learners are expected to inspect FSM representations, detect **structural and behavioral patterns**, and apply **analytical and critical reasoning** to interpret system behavior, justify simplification or refinement decisions, and support design choices grounded in formal modeling principles.

By emphasizing **pattern recognition and abstraction**, this competency enables students to reason beyond individual states and transitions and to identify higher-level regularities that inform effective FSM analysis and design.



### **Knowledge Specification**

The following knowledge areas are essential for demonstrating this competency:

* **Finite State Machines (FSMs)**
  Understanding the structural properties of FSMs—including states, transitions, determinism, equivalence, and minimization—and how these elements affect the representation, analysis, and optimization of computational behavior.

* **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)**
  Applying logical reasoning and pattern recognition to identify underlying structures, draw evidence-based inferences, and support informed decisions during FSM analysis and refinement.



### **Disposition Specification**

The identification of patterns in FSMs requires the following behavioral dispositions:

* **Analytical** — systematically examine state–transition structures and behavioral regularities;
* **Meticulous** — attend to detail when comparing states, transitions, and input patterns;
* **Creative** — recognize alternative abstractions or simplifications of FSM structures;
* **Inventive** — propose refined or optimized representations based on identified patterns.



### **Knowledge–Skill Pairing**

To demonstrate this competency, learners must be able to:

* **Analyze** knowledge of **Finite State Machines** to examine state architectures, identify recurring transition patterns, and evaluate how input classifications affect model structure and complexity.

* **Apply** **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)** to infer structural regularities, justify interpretations of FSM behavior, and support refinement or optimization decisions grounded in formal reasoning.



### **Bloom’s Taxonomy Alignment**

This competency is aligned with **Bloom’s Revised Taxonomy** at two complementary cognitive levels:

* **Finite State Machines — Analyze**
  Learners analyze FSM structures to compare states, transitions, and behavioral patterns.

* **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK) — Apply**
  Learners apply structured reasoning to interpret identified patterns and justify modeling decisions.



### **Verb Annotation**

The following verbs define the expected observable actions associated with this competency:

* **Analyze → Finite State Machines**
  *Examine, Evaluate, Compare*

* **Apply → Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)**
  *Infer, Interpret, Justify*
