# **Specialized Competency Specification — C08.01**

## **Competency C08.01 — Specialized Form of C08**

## **1. Specialization Statement (Formal Justification)**

**Competency C08.01** is a **specialization of Competency C08 – *Identify Turing Machine Variants***, enacted in the context of **Task 05 – *The Farmer Robot and the Feeder Robot***.

While **C08** focuses on the **conceptual recognition and differentiation** of Turing Machine variants in isolation, **C08.01** captures an **expanded interpretative performance**, in which learners must **analyze and compare variants within a system-level and multi-agent modeling context**.

This specialization reflects the need to reason about **variant suitability not only as abstract models**, but as **components within an integrated computational architecture**, without yet engaging in full model construction or implementation.



## **2. Competency Title**

    Analyze the System-Level Adequacy of Turing Machine Variants



## **3. Textual Description (Specialized)**

This competency involves the ability to **analyze, compare, and interpret the adequacy of different Turing Machine variants**—such as **standard**, **multi-tape**, and **non-deterministic models**—when employed within **integrated and multi-agent computational systems**.

Learners must be able to **examine how the structural characteristics of each variant** impact **coordination, modularization, and information flow** across interacting components. Demonstrating this competency requires reasoning about **trade-offs in model organization, clarity, and expressive convenience**, rather than about execution efficiency or theoretical equivalence.

The competency emphasizes **system-level analytical reasoning**, positioning Turing Machine variants as **design-relevant modeling choices** within a unified computational framework, while deliberately avoiding implementation, optimization, or metatheoretical proof.



## **4. Cognitive Scope and Bloom Alignment**

| Aspect               | C08 (Base)                      | C08.01 (Specialized)                          |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Dominant Bloom Level | **Understand**                  | **Analyze**                                   |
| Cognitive Focus      | Recognition and differentiation | System-level comparison and adequacy analysis |
| Modeling Context     | Isolated / abstract             | Integrated, multi-agent system                |
| Decision Nature      | Conceptual classification       | Analytical evaluation of suitability          |

C08.01 explicitly **elevates the dominant cognitive process** from **Understand** to **Analyze**, while remaining **non-productive** (i.e., not Create), thus preserving its distinct role between **C08** and **C09.01**.



## **5. Knowledge Specification (Specialized)**

The following knowledge areas characterize C08.01 as a specialization of C08, refining inherited knowledge for a system-level and multi-agent analytical context.

* **Turing Machine Variants (System-Level Perspective)**
  Knowledge of Turing Machine variants with emphasis on their **structural implications** when used in **integrated or coordinated systems**, including considerations of modularity, separation of concerns, and interaction complexity.

* **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)**
  Ability to analyze integrated computational scenarios, compare alternative modeling choices, and reason about the **adequacy and implications** of different variants within a system-level context.



## **6. Knowledge–Skill Pairing (Specialized)**

### **Mapping Knowledge to Skills**

To demonstrate this competency, learners must be able to:

* **Analyze** integrated computational scenarios to **compare Turing Machine variants**, identifying how their structural characteristics affect coordination, modularity, and clarity of system behavior.

* **Analyze** problem constraints and modeling requirements to **evaluate the adequacy** of different variants as components of a unified computational architecture.

* **Analyze** integrated modeling situations using Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK) to justify comparative judgments, relate system constraints to formal alternatives, and evaluate trade-offs between Turing Machine variants.



## **7. Verb Annotation (Specialized)**

* **Analyze** → *Turing Machine Variants (system-level)*
  **Verbs:** *Compare, Examine, Differentiate*

* **Analyze** → *Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)*
  **Verbs:** *Evaluate, Justify, Relate*



## **8. Relationship to Base Competency ([Ontology] Perspective)**

Formally:

```text
C08.01 ⊑ C08
```

Where:

* **C08** defines the **foundational conceptual capability** to identify and differentiate Turing Machine variants;
* **C08.01** specializes this capability for **analytical evaluation of variant adequacy** within **integrated and multi-agent modeling contexts**.

All knowledge and skills of C08 are **inherited**, with **elevated cognitive expectations** and **contextual constraints** introduced by the specialization.
