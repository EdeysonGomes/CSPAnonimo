## **Competency Lifecycle – C14 – *Differentiate classifications of formal grammars***

#### Competency Title

  Differentiate classifications of formal grammars

### Textual Description (Aprimorada)

This competency refers to the ability to **understand, compare, and differentiate formal grammar classes**, particularly **regular grammars** and **context-free grammars**, in relation to their **structural properties**, **expressive power**, and **associated computational models** (e.g., finite automata and pushdown automata).

Learners are expected to analyze the **formal characteristics and limitations** of each grammar class and to **justify their suitability** for modeling different categories of symbolic or language-based systems. The competency emphasizes **conceptual classification and analytical comparison**, supporting informed decisions about the **appropriate level of formalism** required for system specification or analysis.

This competency draws on the **Chomsky hierarchy as a classificatory reference framework**, enabling learners to reason about grammatical complexity without engaging in the construction or interpretation of specific rule sets.



### **Knowledge Specification (Aprimorada)**

The following knowledge areas are essential for mastering this competency:

* **Context-Free Grammars and Languages**
  *Understand the defining properties of context-free grammars, including production rules, derivation structures, and their ability to represent hierarchical or nested patterns. Recognize their expressive limits and the role of **pushdown automata** as the corresponding recognizing model.*

* **Regular Grammars and Languages**
  *Understand the defining characteristics of regular grammars and languages, including their structural simplicity and correspondence with **finite automata**. Identify the limitations of regular formalisms when compared to context-free grammars.*

* **Analytical and Critical Thinking**
  *Apply formal reasoning to compare grammar classes based on structure, expressive power, and computational requirements. Justify the classification of problems and the selection of appropriate grammatical formalisms using well-defined formal criteria.*



### **Knowledge–Skill Pairing**

#### **Mapping Knowledge to Skills**

To effectively demonstrate this competency, students must be able to:

* **Understand** knowledge of **Context-Free Languages**
  *Associated verbs:* *Recognize, Describe, Differentiate*
  Learners should recognize the defining structural characteristics of context-free grammars, describe their derivation mechanisms and hierarchical patterns, and differentiate their expressive capabilities from those of regular languages.

* **Understand** knowledge of **Regular Languages**
  *Associated verbs:* *Identify, Classify, Compare*
  Learners must identify the core features of regular grammars and their correspondence with finite automata, classify language constructs according to grammatical complexity, and compare regular and context-free languages in terms of expressive power and modeling scope.

* **Apply** **Analytical and Critical Thinking**
  Learners should apply formal reasoning to analyze the suitability of different grammar classes for given modeling scenarios, contrast their structural and computational properties, and justify the selection of an appropriate grammatical formalism based on explicit criteria such as expressiveness, memory requirements, and system constraints.



### **Bloom’s Taxonomy Alignment**

This competency engages the following levels of cognitive processing in a structured progression:

* **Understand** — for identifying, describing, classifying, and differentiating grammar types and their formal properties.
* **Apply** — for analyzing modeling requirements and justifying the selection of appropriate grammar classes based on formal criteria and system-level constraints.

This alignment reflects the **analytical and classificatory nature** of the competency, without extending into model construction or rule interpretation.
