# Specialized Competency Specification — C07.01

## Competency C07.01 — Specialized Form of C07



## 1. Specialization Statement (Formal Justification)

**Competency C07.01** is a **specialization of Competency C07 – *Develop Problem-Solving Solutions Using Turing Machines***, enacted in the context of **Task 05 – *The Farmer Robot and the Feeder Robot***.

While C07 focuses on the **development of problem-solving solutions using Turing Machines in a locally scoped, single-system context**, C07.01 captures an **expanded and specialized form of performance**, characterized by:

* **multi-agent coordination**;
* **integration of multiple interacting computational modules**;
* **use of Turing Machines as a universal modeling framework**, rather than merely an adequate formalism.

This specialization reflects a **controlled elevation of cognitive demand and modeling scope**, without redefining the foundational competency.



## **2. Competency Title**

  Develop Integrated Multi-Agent Solutions Using Turing Machines



## **3. Textual Description (Specialized)**

This competency involves the ability to **design, integrate, and validate coordinated computational solutions** using **Turing Machines** as a **unified and universal formal model**, in scenarios involving **multiple interacting agents and subsystems**.

Learners must be able to **decompose a complex system into interacting components**, define the behavior of each component using Turing Machines, and **integrate these models into a coherent system-level solution**. This includes coordinating shared memory usage, synchronizing signaling logic, and ensuring consistent global behavior across agents.

Demonstrating this competency requires **system-level reasoning**, combining **formal modeling**, **architectural integration**, and **validation of emergent behavior**. The focus is not only on the correctness of individual machines, but on the **coherence, robustness, and adequacy of the integrated solution** within a unified computational framework.



## 4. Cognitive Scope and Bloom Alignment

| Aspect               | C07 (Base)                      | C07.01 (Specialized)                         |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Dominant Bloom Level | **Create**                      | **Create + Analyze**                       |
| Modeling Scope       | Single problem / local behavior | Integrated, multi-agent system             |
| Reasoning Focus      | Construction of a TM solution   | Architectural integration and coordination |
| Validation           | Local execution/simulation      | System-level consistency and interaction   |

C07.01 explicitly **extends** the cognitive scope of C07 by introducing **Analyze** as a dominant supporting process, required for integration, coordination, and architectural reasoning.



## 5. Knowledge Specification (Specialized)

The following knowledge areas are required to demonstrate **C07.01**, in addition to those inherited from C07:

* **Turing Machines (Universal Modeling Perspective)**
  Knowledge of Turing Machines as a **universal representational framework**, supporting the integration of heterogeneous behaviors under a single formal model.

* **System Integration and Coordination Concepts**
  Understanding of coordination mechanisms, signaling protocols, shared memory abstractions, and interaction patterns between autonomous computational agents.

* **Requirements Engineering (System-Level)**
  Ability to elicit, analyze, and formalize **global system requirements**, including inter-agent constraints, coordination rules, and consistency conditions.

* **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)**
  Capacity to analyze interactions between components, reason about emergent behavior, and evaluate the adequacy of the integrated system.


## 6. Knowledge–Skill Pairing (Specialized)

### Mapping Knowledge to Skills

To demonstrate this competency, learners must be able to:

* **Create** integrated computational solutions by **constructing and coordinating multiple Turing Machines**, ensuring consistent global behavior across agents.

* **Apply** system-level **Requirements Engineering** to translate global constraints into formal modeling decisions.

* Analyze system-level behavior using **Analytical and Critical Thinking (FPK)** to justify architectural choices and validate integrated behavior.

* **Analyze** system interactions to identify dependencies, synchronization needs, and potential inconsistencies arising from multi-agent coordination.


## 7. Verb Annotation (Specialized)

* **Create** → *Turing Machines (integration)*
  **Verbs:** *Design, Integrate, Construct*

* **Analyze** → *System Coordination*
  **Verbs:** *Decompose, Examine, Evaluate*

* **Apply** → *Requirements Engineering*
  **Verbs:** *Formalize, Align, Constrain*



## **8. Relationship to Base Competency ([Ontology] Perspective)**

Formally:

```text
C07.01 ⊑ C07
```

Where:

* **C07** defines the **foundational capability** to develop TM-based solutions;
* **C07.01** specializes this capability for **integrated, multi-agent, system-level modeling contexts**.

This specialization preserves **inheritance of all C07 knowledge and skills**, while introducing **additional constraints and elevated cognitive expectations**.
